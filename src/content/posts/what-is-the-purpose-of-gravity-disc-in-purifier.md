<p>Gravity Disk in Purifier&nbsp;<strong>alters the location of the oil-water interface</strong> and is <strong>responsible for creating an interface between the oil and water</strong>. </p>



<p>And further, the Interface can be shifted inwards or outwards with the correct selection of the gravity disk in your purifier. And With the proper selection of the gravity disk, the efficiency of the separation can also be improved.</p>



<p>The gravity disc with proper inner diameter i.e. with the diameter that corresponds to the difference in densities of the oil-water mixture to be treated should, therefore, be inserted in the bowl, This disc can be chosen from the set of discs provided with the separator.</p>



<p>The inner diameter of the disc to be chosen can be determined by: Calculation  Experimen tThe general rule is : Small diameter gravity disc when treating heavy oil Large diameter regulating ring when treating light oil</p>



<p>Selection of correct size of gravity disc is very important to obtain optimum cleaning of oil according to the density of the medium to be treated and throughput.</p>



<p>The diameter if the gravity disk&nbsp;is the innermost circle diameter and water flows it through the inside.</p>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading">What will happen if the size of gravity disc is large?</h2>



<p>The oil will overflow. A larger gravity disk of bigger diameter causes oil flowing out of the purifier Through the water outlet. This is because the interface will shift outside as the pressure will decrease from water outlet side. </p>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading">What if Gravity disk is small?</h2>



<p>Smaller Gravity disk may result in <strong>lesser purifying efficiency</strong> as the outer periphery of the disk stacks is left unused. Also there may be chances of water going out with oil from the oil outlet of purifier</p>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading">How to select the correct size of gravity disc of purifier on ship?</h2>



<p>A correct size of gravity disc is selected based on the specific density of oil, feed rate and purification temperature with the help of <strong>Nomogram </strong>diagram. It is a diagram that shows the Feed rate, temperature, and gravity disk diameter that has to be selected. </p>



<p><strong>Nomogram diagram</strong>s are different for <strong>different types of purifier models</strong>&nbsp;from the same manufacturer. They given in the purifier manual</p>



<p></p>

<p>To Find the correct size of Gravity disc, you must know either specific gravity of treated oil at 15℃ or specific gravity of treated oil at a certain&nbsp;temperature is known</p>



<p><strong>Case 1: Specific gravity of treated oil at 15℃.</strong></p>



<ul>
<li>Specific gravity of treated oil:&nbsp;<strong>0.944</strong> at <strong>50℃</strong></li>



<li>Treating temperature: <strong>98℃</strong></li>



<li>Feed rate: <strong>1250 L/h</strong></li>
</ul>



<ol>
<li>Find the intersection between the falling curve of your specific gravity and the vertical line of your treating temperature.</li>



<li>Draw a horizontal line from that intersection to reach the vertical line of 100℃.</li>



<li>Connect the right end of that line with the point of your feed rate on the treating capacity scale using a straight line.</li>



<li>Read within which section of the gravity disc inside diameter scale the intersection made by that line falls.</li>



<li>Select a gravity disc having that inside diameter.</li>
</ol>



<p>For example, if your specific gravity is 0.925 at 15℃, your treating temperature is 70℃, and your feed rate is 3000 L/h, then you can use the nomogram to find that you need a gravity disc with an inside diameter of φ79</p>
