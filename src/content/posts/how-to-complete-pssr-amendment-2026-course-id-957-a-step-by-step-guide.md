<p>The Directorate General of Shipping (DGS) has introduced a mandatory update for Indian seafarers: the <strong>PSSR Amendment 2026 course (Course ID 957)</strong>, which acts as a key <strong>pssr refresher course</strong>. This new e-learning module is essential for staying compliant with the latest STCW standards.</p>



<figure class="wp-block-image"><img/></figure>



<p>In this guide, we’ll walk you through why this update is necessary, who needs it, and how to complete your <strong>PSSR e-learning</strong> to get your certificate.</p>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading">What is the PSSR Amendment 2026?</h2>



<p>The International Maritime Organization (IMO) has updated the STCW framework to include mandatory training on preventing and responding to violence, harassment, bullying, and sexual assault onboard.</p>



<p>The <strong>PSSR Amendment 2026</strong> is a 3-hour digital module designed to update existing seafarers on these social responsibilities.</p>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Who Must Take the PSSR Amendment 2026 Course?</h2>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Existing Seafarers:</strong> If you completed your original PSSR course before <strong>December 15, 2025</strong>, you MUST complete this 3-hour amendment module.</li>



<li><strong>Freshers:</strong> If you are taking the Basic Safety Training (BST) for the first time after December 2025, the amendment is already included in your 29-hour PSSR course. You do not need to take it separately.</li>
</ul>



<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading">How to Complete PSSR E-Learning (Course 957)</h2>



<p>Follow these simple steps to complete your training on the DGS portal:</p>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Step 1: Enroll on the DGS Portal</h3>



<ol start="1" class="wp-block-list">
<li>Visit the official <a href="https://dgshipping.gov.in/" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">DG Shipping E-Learning website</a>.</li>



<li>Ensure your Seafarer Profile (Photo, Signature, and Phone/Email) is up to date.</li>



<li>Click on <strong>Modular Courses / E-Learning</strong>.</li>



<li>Enter your INDoS Number and Date of Birth.</li>



<li>Select <strong>Course 957 – PSSR Amendment 2026 (PSSRAMDT)</strong> to enroll.</li>
</ol>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Step 2: Complete the Learning Modules</h3>



<p>Once enrolled, go to "My Courses" and select Course 957. The <strong>PSSR e-learning</strong> consists of:</p>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Video lessons and PPT slides.</li>



<li>Reading materials on behavioral safety.</li>



<li>Short practice quizzes (unlimited attempts).</li>
</ul>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Step 3: Pass the Final Assessment</h3>



<p>To earn your certificate, you must pass the final online exit exam.</p>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Passing Score:</strong> 50%</li>



<li><strong>Duration:</strong> Approximately 3 hours of total learning time.</li>
</ul>



<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading">PSSR Amendment 2026 Certificate Download</h2>



<p>Once you have successfully passed the assessment, your digital certificate will be generated. Here is how to access it:</p>



<ol start="1" class="wp-block-list">
<li>Log out of the e-learning portal and wait for approximately <strong>2 hours</strong> for the system to sync.</li>



<li>Visit the DGS website and navigate to the <strong>Certificate Download</strong> section.</li>



<li>Re-enter your INDoS Number and Date of Birth.</li>



<li>Click on <strong>PSSR Amendment 2026 certificate download</strong> to save the PDF.</li>
</ol>



<p><strong>Note:</strong> You must carry this certificate along with your original PSSR certificate when joining your next vessel.</p>



<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Why is this Amendment Mandatory?</h2>



<p>The maritime industry is shifting focus toward psychological safety and a respectful workplace. Failing to complete the <strong>PSSR Amendment 2026 course</strong> may result in:</p>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Being declared "non-compliant" during Port State Control (PSC) inspections.</li>



<li>Delays in joining your next vessel.</li>



<li>Rejection of your documents by shipping companies.</li>
</ul>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Relationship with PSSR Refresher Course & STCW Convention</h3>
<p>The pssr refresher course and the PSSR Amendment 2026 are part of the broader safety updates mandated under the STCW convention. The STCW convention regulates the minimum training standards for all seafaring personnel. Staying up to date with your personal safety and social responsibilities pssr updates ensures that you are fully certified and ready to sail without administrative delays.</p>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)</h2>



<p><strong>Q: Is there a fee for the PSSR Amendment 2026 course?</strong></p>



<p>A: Currently, this is a free e-learning module provided by DG Shipping for all Indian seafarers.</p>



<p><strong>Q: Can I do this course on my mobile?</strong></p>



<p>A: Yes, the DGS e-learning portal is accessible via mobile browsers, though a laptop is recommended for a better experience during the final assessment.</p>



<p><strong>Q: How long is the certificate valid?</strong></p>



<p>A: The amendment certificate does not have an expiry date; it permanently updates your existing PSSR qualification to the 2026 standards.</p>
