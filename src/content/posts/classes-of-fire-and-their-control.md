<p>For effective fire fighting on board a ship, thorough knowledge of the classification of fires is required.&nbsp;</p>



<p>Fires are classified into six types depending on the material, which acts as fuel to start a fire as per the international Organisation&nbsp;for Standardization.</p>



<p><br>As per&nbsp;&nbsp;National Fire Protection Association&nbsp;(NFPA). This is followed in the United States of America. They are:</p>



<p><br><strong>Class A</strong>: Result from ordinary combustible materials, including wood, cloth, paper, and many plastics. </p>



<div class="wp-block-image"><figure class="aligncenter size-full is-resized"><img src="/wp-content/uploads/2021/08/Class-A-Fire.png" alt="" class="wp-image-136" width="502" height="303"/><figcaption>Class A fire</figcaption></figure></div>



<p><strong>Class B</strong>: Burn inflammable liquids, combustible liquids, petroleum greases, oils, alcohols, and flammable gases. </p>



<div class="wp-block-image"><figure class="aligncenter size-full is-resized"><img src="/wp-content/uploads/2021/08/Class-B-Fire.png" alt="" class="wp-image-138" width="501" height="273"/><figcaption>Class B</figcaption></figure></div>



<p><strong>Class C</strong>: Involve energized electrical equipment. </p>



<div class="wp-block-image"><figure class="aligncenter size-full is-resized"><img src="/wp-content/uploads/2021/08/Class-C-fire.png" alt="" class="wp-image-139" width="443" height="258"/><figcaption>Class C</figcaption></figure></div>



<p><strong>Class D</strong>: Fires in combustible metals, such as magnesium, titanium, zirconium, sodium, lithium, and potassium. </p>



<div class="wp-block-image"><figure class="aligncenter size-full is-resized"><img src="/wp-content/uploads/2021/08/Class-D-Fire.png" alt="" class="wp-image-141" width="418" height="233"/><figcaption>Class D</figcaption></figure></div>



<p><strong>Class K</strong>: Fires in cooking appliances that involve combustible cooking media, such as vegetable or animal oils and fats.</p>



<div class="wp-block-image"><figure class="aligncenter size-full is-resized"><img src="/wp-content/uploads/2021/08/Class-K-Fire.png" alt="" class="wp-image-142" width="441" height="248"/><figcaption>Class K</figcaption></figure></div>



<p>In India ISO standards are followed. They are:</p>



<p><br><strong>Class A fires – Combustible materials:</strong> caused by flammable solids, such as wood, paper, and fabric</p>



<p><strong>Class B fires – Flammable liquids:</strong> such as petrol, turpentine or paint</p>



<p><strong>Class C fires – Flammable gases:</strong>&nbsp;like hydrogen, butane or methane</p>



<p><strong>Class D fires – Combustible&nbsp;metals</strong>:&nbsp;chemicals such as magnesium, aluminium or potassiumElectrical&nbsp;fires – Electrical equipment:&nbsp;once the electrical item is removed, the fire&nbsp;changes class</p>



<p><strong>Class F fires – Cooking oils:</strong>&nbsp;typically a chip-pan fire.</p>



<p>There are six different classes of fire that can be prevented by using a different fire extinguisher. </p>
