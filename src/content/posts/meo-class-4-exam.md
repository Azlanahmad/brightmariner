<p>Hello Everyone, now we are going to discuss the first competency exam for assistant marine engineer officers, TMEs, and engine cadets—the MEO Class 4 examination conducted by MMD under DG Shipping.</p>



<p>MEO stands for Marine Engineering/Engineer Officer. This qualification is conducted by DG Shipping through the Marine Mercantile Department (MMD).</p>



<h2><strong>Eligibility Requirements for Class 4 Competency</strong></h2>



<p># <strong>Sailing period:</strong></p>



<p>For Motor – a minimum of 6 months (183 days)</p>



<p>For Steam – a minimum of 6 months (183 days)</p>



<p>For combined Steam &amp; Motor – 8 months (4 months on Motor + 4 months on Steam)</p>



<p># <strong>Propulsion period:</strong></p>



<p><strong>For Motor</strong>: a minimum of 4 months (121 days)</p>



<p><strong>For Steam</strong>: a minimum of 4 months (121 days)</p>



<p><strong>For combined Steam &amp; Motor</strong>: 6 months (3 months on Motor + 3 months on Steam)</p>



<p>* Engine Power: Main Engine of power minimum 750KW or more.</p>



<p>* A sea service letter is required to apply for the exam</p>



<h2><br><strong>Mandatory Courses Before Applying</strong></h2>



<p>The Directorate has decided to make this ELeaming course mandatory to be completed by all candidates prior to appearing for the MEO Class IV examination at no additional cost to the candidate. </p>



<p>After 6 months of onboard training, all candidates should complete the following 4 courses:</p>



<ol><li>Medical First Aid (MFA)</li><li>Advanced Fire Fighting (AFF)</li><li>Proficiency in Survival Craft and Rescue Boats (PSCRB)</li><li>Engine Room Simulator (ERS)</li></ol>



<h2><strong>Certificates Required to Register</strong></h2>



<ol><li>Passport</li><li>CDC with the proper entry of sea service details.</li><li>Class 10th Certificate</li><li>10+2 / diploma Certificate</li><li>Graduation Certificate</li><li>Pre-Sea Training Certificate</li><li>Sea Service Letter From the company with proper details and signature</li><li>Sea Service Testimonials (you can contact the company if you missed the same)</li><li>All STCW Certificates</li><li>Advanced Modular Course Certificates</li></ol>



<h2><strong>Exam Subjects &amp; Functions</strong></h2>



<p>MEO Class 4 examination consists of 2 parts :(Part A + Part B)</p>



<p>Part B is compulsory for all candidates, but Part A is exempted for Graduated (GME/ME) candidates.</p>



<p>Part: B (For all candidates including GME and BTech)</p>

<p>Total 10 Papers : 6 written + 4 orals</p>



<h3>4 Oral Papers:</h3>



<ul><li>Function 3:- Controlling Operations of ships &amp; care for persons on board at the operational level (COSCPOOL)</li><li>Function 4:-Marine Engineering at Operational Level (MEOL)</li><li>Function 5:- Electronics and Control Engineering at Operational Level (EECEOL)</li><li>Function 6:- Maintenance &amp; Repair at Operational Level (MROL)</li><li></li></ul>



<h3>6 Written Examination Papers are:</h3>



<ol><li>Marine Engineering Knowledge: General</li><li>Marine Engineering Knowledge: Motor</li><li>Ship Safety &amp; Environment Protection</li><li>Marine Engineering Practice</li><li>Ship Construction and stability</li><li>Marine Electro technology</li></ol>

<h2>Marine Engineering Career Path & Class 4 COC</h2>

<p>Earning your <strong>class 4 coc</strong> (Certificate of Competency) is the crucial first milestone in a professional marine engineer's career. After completing your pre-sea <strong>meo course</strong> at a DGS-approved maritime training institute, you undergo junior engineering shipboard training. </p>

<p>As you accumulate the required sea service and propelling time on vessels with main engines of at least <strong>750 kw</strong>, you become eligible to sit for the competency assessments. This qualification allows you to sail as a certified Officer in Charge of an Engineering Watch (OICNW). From there, you can pursue further qualifications, including the <strong>meo class ii</strong> and eventually the Class I exams, paving the way to becoming a chief engineer. Selecting the right maritime training institute for preparatory classes helps you pass the oral and written examinations on your first attempt.</p>
