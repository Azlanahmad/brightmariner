<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>How to Check if an Auxiliary Engine Rocker Arm is Properly Lubricated</strong></h3>



<p>To determine whether the rocker arm on an auxiliary engine is being lubricated, follow these steps:</p>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Listen for the sound</strong>: Stand near the rocker arm (maintaining a safe distance). When the rocker arm is properly lubricated, it emits a <strong>low-pitched sound</strong>. If lubrication is lacking, the sound will be <strong>loud and high-pitched</strong>. Proper lubrication is critical for engine performance and preventing wear.</li>
</ul>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Essential Preparations for Machinery Overhaul</strong></h3>



<p>Before overhauling any machinery, it’s crucial to prepare thoroughly to ensure safety and efficiency. Here's a checklist of necessary preparations:</p>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Toolbox Meeting</strong>: Discuss tools (including special tools), lighting, ventilation, and spare parts availability.</li>



<li><strong>Time Management &amp; Manpower</strong>: Organize tasks, assign personnel, and ensure efficient use of time.</li>



<li><strong>Risk Assessment</strong>: Conduct a risk assessment and complete the necessary checklist.</li>



<li><strong>Work Area Safety</strong>: Secure the working area with signs and barricades. Obtain the required work permits.</li>



<li><strong>Record Readings</strong>: Document relevant readings and records before starting the overhaul.</li>
</ul>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Routine Maintenance for Auxiliary Engines</strong></h3>



<p>Proper maintenance of auxiliary engines is essential for smooth operation. Here's a breakdown of daily, 250-hour, 1000-hour, 2000-hour, 4000-hour, and 8000-hour routines:</p>



<h4 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Daily Engine Checks</strong></h4>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Check the fuel level in the service tank and drain water from the service and settling tanks.</li>



<li>Inspect lube oil levels in the sump and governor.</li>



<li>Verify rocker arm lubrication and lube oil pressure.</li>



<li>Check water pressure, exhaust temperature, and cooling water temperature.</li>



<li>Drain condensate from the scavenge manifold and air coolers.</li>



<li>Lubricate fuel racks, linkages, and pump rack operating gear.</li>
</ul>



<h4 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>250-Hour Maintenance Routine</strong></h4>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Change over generators and stop the engine.</li>



<li>Clean the lube oil filters, fuel oil filters, and turbocharger air filters.</li>
</ul>



<h4 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>1000-Hour Maintenance Routine</strong></h4>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Perform crankcase inspection.</li>



<li>Pressure test and adjust fuel injectors.</li>



<li>Change the turbocharger blower and turbine-side oil.</li>



<li>Check tappet clearances and engine performance.</li>
</ul>



<h4 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>2000-Hour Maintenance Routine</strong></h4>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Check crankshaft alignment and plot graphs to compare with original figures.</li>



<li>Ensure the engines are aligned to maintain optimal performance of the alternator bearings.</li>
</ul>



<h4 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>4000-Hour Maintenance Routine (Half Decarbonization)</strong></h4>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Recondition cylinder heads and remove carbon deposits.</li>



<li>Inspect and lap exhaust and inlet valves.</li>



<li>Overhaul starting air valves and renew gaskets and O-rings.</li>



<li>Clean the cooling water spaces of the cylinder heads.</li>



<li>Inspect piston tops and cylinder liner for combustion abnormalities.</li>
</ul>



<h4 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>8000-Hour Maintenance Routine (Full Decarbonization)</strong></h4>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Overhaul all pistons, change piston rings, and clean exhaust piping.</li>



<li>Inspect big-end bearings and overhaul the turbocharger.</li>



<li>Renew ball bearings and clean the silencer.</li>
</ul>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Common Reasons for Generator Failure to Start</strong></h3>



<p>If a generator fails to start, several factors could be responsible. Here's a list of common issues:</p>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Low air bottle pressure or closed air line valves.</li>



<li>Malfunction of air bottle isolating valve or distributor.</li>



<li>Faulty control of air valves or insufficient control of air pressure.</li>



<li>Jammed start air automatic valve or engaged turning gear.</li>



<li>Faulty limit switch or control valve not in its end position.</li>



<li>The auxiliary blower not running or not in auto mode.</li>



<li>Low oil pressure due to an open exhaust valve or weak spring pressure.</li>



<li>Defective or sticky cylinder air start valves.</li>
</ul>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Actions to Take for High Lube Oil Consumption After Generator Decarbonization</strong></h3>



<p>After completing decarbonization, if the lube oil consumption is abnormally high, take the following actions:</p>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Inspect all pipes and connections for leaks.</li>



<li>Check if the lube oil return valve from the crankcase to the sump tank is properly closed.</li>
</ul>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>How to Safely Remove a Stuck Hydraulic Jack</strong></h3>



<p>If a hydraulic jack becomes stuck, follow these steps to safely remove it:</p>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Ensure that the loosened nut moves freely and does not contact the piston.</li>



<li>Slightly increase the hydraulic pressure and turn the nut 1/4 turn clockwise to loosen it from the piston.</li>



<li>After loosening, release the hydraulic pressure and dismount the jack.</li>
</ul>
