            <div class="grid md:grid-cols-2 gap-8 items-center">
                <div class="fade-in-up" style="transition-delay: 200ms;">
                    <h4 class="text-2xl font-semibold text-white mb-4">Volumetric Energy Density</h4>
                    <p class="mb-6">Ammonia's key advantage is its zero-carbon molecular structure. However, its low energy density is a major design hurdle, requiring tank volumes ~3x larger than those for HFO to store the same amount of energy.</p>
                    <canvas id="fuelPropertiesChart"></canvas>
                </div>
                <div class="space-y-6">
                    <div class="glass-card p-6 rounded-lg fade-in-up" style="transition-delay: 400ms;">
                        <h5 class="text-xl font-bold text-sky-400 mb-2">The Ignition Challenge</h5>
                        <p>With a high autoignition temperature (~650°C) and slow flame speed, ammonia cannot be ignited by compression alone. This would require impractical compression ratios of 35:1 or more, forcing the adoption of dual-fuel strategies.</p>
                    </div>
                    <div class="glass-card p-6 rounded-lg fade-in-up" style="transition-delay: 600ms;">
                        <h5 class="text-xl font-bold text-sky-400 mb-2">Material Hurdles</h5>
                        <p>Ammonia is corrosive to copper, zinc, and their alloys. It can also cause Stress Corrosion Cracking (SCC) in certain steels. All fuel-wetted components must be made of stainless steel or specialized materials.</p>
                    </div>
                    <div class="glass-card p-6 rounded-lg fade-in-up" style="transition-delay: 800ms;">
                        <h5 class="text-xl font-bold text-sky-400 mb-2">Thermodynamic Hurdles</h5>
                        <p>Ammonia has a high heat of vaporization. When injected as a liquid, it absorbs significant heat from the cylinder, creating a charge cooling effect that further inhibits the already difficult ignition and slows combustion.</p>
                    </div>
                </div>
            </div>

        <section id="emissions" class="py-20">
            <div class="section-header mb-12 fade-in-up">
                <h3 class="text-4xl font-bold text-white">Managing the Exhaust Stream</h3>
                <p class="text-lg text-sky-300">Ammonia eliminates CO₂ but introduces new emissions that require treatment.</p>
            </div>
            <div class="space-y-12">
                <div class="grid md:grid-cols-3 gap-8 fade-in-up">
                    <div class="glass-card p-6 rounded-lg">
                        <h5 class="text-xl font-bold text-sky-400 mb-2">Nitrogen Oxides (NOₓ)</h5>
                        <p>Formed during high-temperature combustion. While significant, levels are manageable with Selective Catalytic Reduction (SCR) systems.</p>
                    </div>
                    <div class="glass-card p-6 rounded-lg">
                        <h5 class="text-xl font-bold text-sky-400 mb-2">Nitrous Oxide (N₂O)</h5>
                        <p>A potent GHG with ~273x the warming potential of CO₂. Formed during incomplete combustion, its abatement is critical to realizing ammonia's climate benefits.</p>
                    </div>
                    <div class="glass-card p-6 rounded-lg">
                        <h5 class="text-xl font-bold text-sky-400 mb-2">Ammonia Slip (NH₃)</h5>
                        <p>Unburned ammonia that "slips" through the engine. It's a fuel loss and a toxic air pollutant that requires its own catalytic treatment (AMOX).</p>
                    </div>
                </div>
                <div class="glass-card p-6 md:p-8 rounded-lg fade-in-up">
                    <h4 class="text-2xl font-semibold text-white mb-6 text-center">Exhaust After-treatment Process</h4>

        <!-- Section 5: Future Technologies -->
        <section id="future-tech" class="py-20">
            <div class="section-header mb-12 fade-in-up">
                <h3 class="text-4xl font-bold text-white">Beyond the Pilot Flame</h3>
                <p class="text-lg text-sky-300">Advanced R&D aims to eliminate pilot fuels and create true mono-fuel ammonia engines.</p>
            </div>
            <div class="grid md:grid-cols-3 gap-8">
                <div class="glass-card p-6 rounded-lg fade-in-up">
                    <h5 class="text-xl font-bold text-sky-400 mb-2">Hydrogen Enhancement</h5>
                    <p>Using ammonia itself as a hydrogen carrier. A small portion is "cracked" in an onboard reactor to produce a hydrogen-rich gas, which is then blended with the main fuel to dramatically improve combustion.</p>
                </div>
                <div class="glass-card p-6 rounded-lg fade-in-up" style="transition-delay: 200ms;">
                    <h5 class="text-xl font-bold text-sky-400 mb-2">Advanced Ignition</h5>
                    <p>Technologies like Turbulent Jet Ignition (TJI) use a small pre-chamber to fire jets of hot, burning gas into the main cylinder, providing a much more energetic and distributed ignition source than a conventional spark plug.</p>
                </div>
                <div class="glass-card p-6 rounded-lg fade-in-up" style="transition-delay: 400ms;">
                    <h5 class="text-xl font-bold text-sky-400 mb-2">Plasma & Laser Ignition</h5>
                    <p>The cutting edge of research. Plasma-assisted systems create reactive chemical species to pre-condition the fuel, while laser systems offer precise control over ignition timing and location within the cylinder.</p>
                </div>
            </div>
        </section>

        const hpdfBtn = document.getElementById('hpdf-btn');
        const lpdfBtn = document.getElementById('lpdf-btn');
        const engineDesc = document.getElementById('engine-desc');
        const pilotSpray = document.getElementById('pilot-spray');
        const hpdfSpray = document.getElementById('hpdf-spray');
        const lpdfPort = document.getElementById('lpdf-port');
        const hpdfInjector = document.getElementById('hpdf-injector');
        
        let engineAnimationInterval;

        function playEngineAnimation(mode) {
            if (engineAnimationInterval) clearInterval(engineAnimationInterval);
            
            pilotSpray.classList.remove('active');
            hpdfSpray.classList.remove('active');
            lpdfPort.classList.remove('active');

            engineAnimationInterval = setInterval(() => {
                pilotSpray.classList.remove('active');
                hpdfSpray.classList.remove('active');
                lpdfPort.classList.remove('active');
                
                setTimeout(() => {
                    pilotSpray.classList.add('active');
                    if (mode === 'hpdf') {
                        hpdfSpray.classList.add('active');
                    } else if (mode === 'lpdf') {
                        lpdfPort.classList.add('active');
                    }
                }, 1000);

            }, 2000);
        }

            hpdfBtn.classList.add('bg-sky-600');
            hpdfBtn.classList.remove('bg-gray-700');
            lpdfBtn.classList.remove('bg-sky-600');
            lpdfBtn.classList.add('bg-gray-700');
            engineDesc.textContent = "HPDF (Diesel Cycle): Liquid ammonia is injected at very high pressure (600-700 bar) directly into the cylinder. This offers precise control and efficiency but requires complex, high-pressure systems.";
            hpdfInjector.style.opacity = '1';
            playEngineAnimation('hpdf');
        });

        lpdfBtn.addEventListener('click', () => {
            lpdfBtn.classList.add('bg-sky-600');
            lpdfBtn.classList.remove('bg-gray-700');
            hpdfBtn.classList.remove('bg-sky-600');
            hpdfBtn.classList.add('bg-gray-700');
            engineDesc.textContent = "LPDF (Otto Cycle): Gaseous ammonia is injected at low pressure into the intake port before entering the cylinder. This uses a simpler fuel system but can have lower power density.";
            hpdfInjector.style.opacity = '0';
            playEngineAnimation('lpdf');
        });
